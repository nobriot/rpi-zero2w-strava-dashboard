# What You Need

## Required hardware

### Waveshare RPi Zero 2W PhotoPainter

This is the all-in-one kit that includes:

- **Raspberry Pi Zero 2W** board
- **7.3-inch 6-color e-paper display** (800 x 480 pixels, shows black, white,
  red, green, blue, and yellow)
- **Carrier board** with battery charger, battery monitor, and real-time clock
- **Photo frame** enclosure

You can buy it from [Waveshare's website](https://www.waveshare.com/rpi-zero-photopainter.htm)
or from electronics retailers like Amazon, AliExpress, or DigiKey.

> **Note:** Make sure you get the version that **includes** the Raspberry Pi
> Zero 2W. Some listings sell only the frame without the Pi.

### MicroSD card

A **16 GB or larger** microSD card. The PhotoPainter kit sometimes comes with
one pre-flashed with Raspberry Pi OS, but it's good to have a spare.

**Recommended:** SanDisk Ultra 32 GB or Samsung EVO Select 32 GB.

### Power supply

A **5V / 2.5A USB-C power adapter** to power the board. It will deliver power
to the RPi.

## Required accounts

### Strava account

You need a [Strava](https://www.strava.com) account with some recorded
activities. A free account works fine, you don't need Strava Summit or a
paid subscription.

## Required software on your computer

You'll need a computer (Mac, Linux, or Windows with WSL) to build the
dashboard software and copy it to the Pi.

There are details in the [Building the Software](./building.md)
chapter.

## Optional

- **micro-USB cable** --- to connect peripherals to the RPi.
- **HDMI mini adapter + monitor** --- useful for initial debugging, but not
  needed if you set up WiFi on the SD card before first boot.
